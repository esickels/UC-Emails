Dear [Student First Name]

We are eager to welcome you back to the IUPUI campus for your sophomore year! Many students feel that there is a pretty dramatic difference between their freshman and sophomore years. While the freshman year is full of exciting ‘firsts’ the sophomore year can feel pretty routine – or even full of anxiety about not being sure which major to pursue or whether you’ll be admitted to your top-choice program. University College is committed to providing you with the support and assistance you need to make a great transition to your second year at IUPUI, and we look forward to your return to campus next month.

Sincerely,

Kathy E. Johnson, Ph.D.
Dean, IUPUI University College

Sophomore Bucket List:

  • Grab some free ice cream at the Ice Cream Social on August 20, 11– 2 PM, University College Courtyard
  • Meet with your academic advisor to develop a course plan from now until graduation. Develop a timeline for moving out of University College and into the IU or Purdue School from which your degree will be granted.
  • Shop for an on-campus job at the Student Employment Part-Time Job Fair on August 22, 10 – 1 PM, University College Courtyard
  • Stop by the Major and Career Information Day (MCID) to meet with students, alumni and staff representatives from IUPUI undergraduate degree programs or to learn about RISE experiences, October 1, 12-2 PM, Campus Center, 450
  • Stay healthy and active! Check out the new bike repair stations across campus, pick up organic vegetables at the Taylor Hall farmers’ market on Tuesday afternoons, or join an intramural sports team
  • Visit the Eiteljorg Museum for free, with your IUPUI Jag Tag
  • Meet with a career professional in University College’s Office of Career Planning to explore career possibilities and create ‘alternative plans’ to maximize your potential for success.
  • Participate in an Externship this year to explore a career you are interested in. Take part in the Excellence in Professionalism series to enhance your resume-writing and interview skills. Have your resume reviewed by staff in the Office of Student Employment.
  • Join a new club! Check out the Office of Student Involvement and find a student organization that enables you to explore a new area of academic study, meet new people, serve community organizations in Indianapolis, and develop leadership skills.
